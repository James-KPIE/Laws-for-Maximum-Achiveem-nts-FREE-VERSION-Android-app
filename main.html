THE LAW OF FELLOWSHIP
The word fellowship is a very strong word. In Greek, it is called “Koinonia,” which means giving and receiving. It is noteworthy that fellowship with God is the greatest privilege He has extended to us, His people.

When God created man in the beginning, the Bible says, “male and female made He them” and He usually came to visit them in the Garden of Eden. To what intent was this visit? Indeed, it was for fellowship.

Eden itself was called a place of fellowship; where life began. After God created Adam, He placed him in Eden, where they had fellowship. This gave Adam the privilege of knowing God's purpose for him.

But, in Genesis chapter three, when Adam fell, he lost the privilege of fellowship with God and became wanting to God as a result. This made God to come looking for him. But when God came, Adam was in hiding. And the LORD God called unto Adam, and said unto him, where art thou? (Genesis 3:9).In response, Adam said, “I heard thy voice in the garden, and I was afraid, because I was naked; and I hid myself” (Genesis. 3:10).

After several years, Jesus Christ came as the last Adam to restore the fellowship with God that the first Adam lost. The privilege of this fellowship therefore is now specially available, but only to those who accept Jesus into their lives as their Saviour and Lord. Authoritatively, Jesus said:

…I am the way, the truth, and the life: no
man cometh unto the father, but by me.
John 14:6.

There had to be a path through which we would return to God, the Father before fellowship with Him can become our experience. That was what Jesus came to make possible. The Cross on which He died for our sins is the bridge that connects us to God.

As Christians, one of our greatest privileges is that privilege of fellowship with God. We must also understand that we are called to fellowship. God, Himself, said: This people have I formed for myself; they shall show forth my praise. Isaiah 43:21.

From this scripture, we understand that we are created by God for Himself; therefore, God desires fellowship with us. This is because, by our fellowship with Him, He will manifest His glory in the world through us. As we come before God in fellowship, He radiates His glory.

FELLOWSHIP BRINGS GLORY
The glory of God is the expression of God's Personality. It is the light of God's countenance. It is the beauty of God's presence. In Greek language, glory is called CHABOD, meaning the Ark of God. God the Creator created His creature - man for His glory. Hence the creature and the Creator cannot fellowship at the same level. The creature's duty in fellowship is that of worship of His Creator. As we fellowship with our Creator - The Almighty God, He in return begins to release His glory upon us. In fellowship, God begins to release the light of His countenance, the beauty of His glorious presence upon our lives. Also, He makes us to have the privilege of being connected to everything He is, so that we can make the most of our lives on earth.

When the glory of God is upon you, it will be so evident that no man can pretend not to see it. For instance, Moses went up on Mount Sinai and had fellowship with God. In the course of that fellowship, the glory cloud of God descended upon Mt. Sinai and there was smoke upon the mountain. That smoke came as a covering, especially to keep Moses in fellowship with God and to shield him from the sight of others. While God communed with Moses, he (Moses) was privileged to see the handwriting of the Almighty and to hear the voice of His majesty. When he descended from the mountain, the first thing the people saw on him was the glory of God that had come upon him, and they couldn't look at his face because of the blazing glory of God upon him. Inside that glory, sickness cannot stay. Inside that glory, anything that causes shame is swallowed up. Inside that glory is the anointing that destroys every form of yoke.

Inside that glory is the wisdom that makes principalities and powers to be subdued. When you come into that moment; where you develop that kind of lifestyle of just getting lost in God's glory cloud, you become a man or woman to be envied, honoured and revered.

Job said, “When men heard of me they were quiet!” That is what happens when God breathes on you; it makes you to breathe divine life out of your own system. Thus, people seek to listen to you.

THE PRICE OF GOD'S GLORY
Those Christians who say, “Let us take it easy; you know< Rome was not built in a day”, will wake up one day only to realize that they have been overtaken by those, who are diligent in their walk with God. Then, they will realize that God is no respecter of persons; He will only take you as far as you are willingly to go.

Therefore, “I got born again in 1938,” is not an achievement. You should be more concerned about being connected to God.
John 15:1 says:

I am the true vine, and my Father is the husband man.

In other words, Jesus is the true Vine. As such it is needful to know that there are counterfeit vines. Therefore beware and be careful! Be diligent to observe, so you don't get deceived! Don't get carried away by activities, sensationalisms and emotions.

Furthermore, Jesus said, “My Father is the husbandman.” The word “husbandman” here means the gardener. This means as the Gardener, God takes away every branch that bears no fruit; but the branch that bears fruit, He purges that it may bring forth more fruit. (John 15:2)

In other words, God is inviting you and me to a superproductive life. How many of us are ready for more fruits, more success, more breakthroughs, more miracles, more promotions, more divine elevations, etc.? It is coming your way in the name of Jesus! How many of us are ready for effective living? The God of heaven is lifting you in the name of Jesus!

John 15:4 says:
Abide in me and I in you, as the branch cannot bear fruit on its own except it abide in the vine. No more can except you abide in me.

The level of success you are going to enjoy in life is going to be determined by your level of intimacy with God. As you know, the closer you are to a mirror, the better you see yourself; the closer you get to fire, the warmer you become; the longer you stand in the rain after a heavy drought, the more lasting you get refreshed. After a long drought, when the rain falls and water gathers somewhere, ducks begin to come around there to swim and in excitement you see them wagging their tails. In the same vein, as you draw close to God, you won't see drought in your life anymore! In His presence you will have joy and pleasures forever more in Jesus mighty name.

The anointing that flows into us (as the branch) through Jesus, the Vine, brings upon us a refreshing that prepares us for a fruitful life. Jesus as the Vine is the Source of life to us. As long as you are still connected to the vine, you will remain a fruitful branch. This is because the closer you are to God, the brighter you will shine in life.

Again, the Bible says, “In His presence is fullness of joy”. Do you want your cup to be full of God's blessings? Then, lift it up to the Lord. Many come with cups that are almost full. But God wants us to come with empty cups, so that we can be filled.

Therefore, be hungry for God. This is because it is hunger for God that makes us desperate and it in turn brings us into God's presence. It is, therefore time to be desperate for God.
